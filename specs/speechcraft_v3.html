Speech at the
Singapore Fintech Festival 2025

Good morning. It is a privilege to join you once again at the Singapore Fintech Festival — a gathering that has come to represent not just the scale of our ambitions, but the depth of trust that underpins everything we seek to build together.5

Singapore is today ranked among the top three fintech hubs in Asia-Pacific.3 That is not an accident. It is the result of sustained, deliberate choices — by government, by industry, and by the many talented individuals who have chosen to build here. But rankings are a lagging indicator. What matters more is what we choose to build next.

Let me share three things on my mind this morning.

First, on support for innovation. In Budget 2025, we enhanced the Enterprise Development Grant to help more fintech firms scale their capabilities and internationalise.2 We also expanded SkillsFuture pathways for digital finance roles, because the talent ecosystem is as important as the regulatory one. And MAS has updated the regulatory sandbox to allow faster, safer testing of new financial products — because we understand that speed matters when innovation waits for no one.4

Second, on the responsible use of AI. Artificial intelligence is already reshaping financial services — in credit assessment, fraud detection, customer service, and beyond. We welcome this. But we also hold a clear position: innovation without accountability is not innovation we can sustain.1

The MAS FEAT principles — Fairness, Ethics, Accountability, and Transparency — are not bureaucratic constraints. They are the foundation on which trust is built.1 And trust, as anyone in financial services knows, is the most valuable and most fragile asset of all.

Third, on partnership. Government can set the conditions. Industry must do the building. What Singapore does well — and what I hope we continue to do — is develop these frameworks together.1 The consultation papers, the industry working groups, the bilateral engagements — these are not theatre. They reflect a genuine belief that the best policy is policy that practitioners have helped to shape.

Our ambition is clear: to be Asia's gateway for trusted digital finance.6 That means being the place where new ideas get tested, refined, and validated before they scale across the region. It means being a jurisdiction where innovation is met not with suspicion, but with a structured, principled welcome.

We have much still to build. But standing here, looking out at this room — the founders, the investors, the policymakers, the builders — I am confident that we will build it well.

Thank you.
